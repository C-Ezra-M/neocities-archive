        # List of Pokémon names in Globasa
        
        This is a list of my own ideas for Pokémon names in Globasa.

        ## Alphabet and pronunciation

        The Globasa alphabet has 25 letters. It doesn't use Q.

        ```
        A B C D E F G H I J K L M N O P R S T U V W X Y Z
        a b c d e f g h i j k l m n o p r s t u v w x y z
        ```

        - Globasa uses a five-vowel system. Therefore, all vowels are pronounced the same as in languages like Spanish, Italian, etc.
        - C is pronounced like CH in *chop*.
        - G is always a hard G.
        - H is pronounced a lot like H in English, but can also be velar, closer to K.
        - R is defined as a tap (/ɾ/), but can in reality be any rhotic sound.
        - X is pronounced like SH in *show*.

        Globasa's phonotactics are simple: (C)(C)V(C). The onset can only be one of the following:
        - Only p/b, f/v, and k/g can be followed by L. Those and t/d can also be followed by R.
        - Anything can be followed by W or Y.

        There cannot be two stops next to each other (like in Italian or Portuguese), and a word cannot start with a sibilant followed by a consonant other than W or Y (which would necessitate placing an e- at the beginning, like Spanish). *wu* and *yi* are not allowed. Words can only end with a vowel or F, L, M, N, R, S, W, X, Y.

        The syllable structure for names is (C)(C)V(C)(C). Anything can be at the end of a word. Also, the combinations *wu*, *yi*, and of two consecutive stops are allowed.

        # Rules for making names
        - They must fit within 12 characters. I don't care if the older games only permit 10. It was a limit placed out of necessity to fit on a Game Boy screen, but later games obviously have more space.
