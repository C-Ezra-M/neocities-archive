---
title: What is a file in computing?
date: 2025-08-03
---

The word for "file" is interesting when you look at other languages. Polish reused the word *plik*, originally used for a stack of papers; the same happened with Arabic مِلَفّ *milaff*, French *fichier*, Portuguese *ficheiro*, Persian پرونده *parvande*.

Icelandic reused the word *skrá* ("manuscript on vellum"). This is similar to how *sími* ("long thread") was reused for the meaning of "telephone".

Many other languages borrowed the term from English: Russian *файл fajl*, Japanese ファイル *fairu*, Korean 파일 *pail*, Italian *file*, Lithuanian *failas*, Georgian ფაილი *paili*.

Armenian created a neologism for that purpose: նիշք *nišk'*, from նիշ- *niš* ("sign, digit, symbol") and -ք *-k'* (noun-forming marker). Hungarian did so too: *adatállomány* comes from *adat* ("data") and *állomány* ("stock"). Both languages also have the term borrowed from English: Hungarian *fájl* and Armenian ֆայլ *fayl*. Another language that created a neologism for this is Vietnamese: *tập tin*.
